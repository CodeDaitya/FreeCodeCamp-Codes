Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man Of India

Former President of India, acclaimed with the title of "Missile Man of India" after successfully leading the Pokhran II, a series of five nuclear bomb test.

Here's a timeline to his life:

  • 1931 : Born in Rameswaram, Karnataka
  • 1954 : Graduated from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappall
  • 1955 : Enroled in Madras Institute of Technology in Aerospace Engineering
  • 1960 : Joined Aeronautical Development Establishment of Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a chief scientist
  • 1969 : Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). Kalam lead many projects and turned out to be successful each time. He played a pivotal role in developing missiles under the missions Agni and Prithvi
  • 1970 : Kalam directed two projects, namely, Project Devil and Project Valiant, which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV programme. A milestone was achieved when locally built Rohini-1 was launched into space, using the SLV rocket
  • 1982 : Chief Executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (I.G.M.D.P) which researched in simultaneous development of a quiver of missiles instead of taking planned missiles one by one
  • 1992 : Appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of Defence Research and Development Organisation
  • 1998 : Chief Co-ordinator of Operation Shakti, a series of five nuclear bom test including one fusion bomb and four fission bombs, after which he was fondly called the “Missile Man of India”
  • 2002 : Kalam succeeded K. R. Narayan to serve as the 11th President of India to become the first scientist to occupy Rashtrapati Bhavan. Occupied ofiice till 2007
  • 2008 : Concluded I.G.M.D.P with the development and testing of it's last missile Agni-3
  • 2011 : Launched his mission for the youth of the nation called the “What Can I Give Movement” with the main aim to defeat corruption in India
  • 2015 : Worked till his last breath, collapsed in the middle of a lecture at Indian Institute Of Management, Shillong
